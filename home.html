Latur –The Cultural Gem of Marathwada

An educational hub, historical city & thriving agricultural centre of Maharashtra.

About Latur

Latur is the administrative headquarter of Latur District in Maharashtra. Situated on the Deccan/​Balaghat Plateau at an elevation of ~631 m, it is known as the ‘Oxford of Maharashtra’ due to its educational institutions :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

The city has ancient roots with the Rashtrakuta dynasty (753–973 CE), founded by King Amoghavarsha — originally known as ‘Lattalur’ :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Top Attractions

Siddheshwar & Ratneshwar Temple

12th‑century Shaivite temple built by King Tamradwaj, famous for its Mahashivratri fair :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Kharosa Caves

6th–7th century rock-cut caves with Buddhist, Hindu & Jain carvings, plus Renuka Devi temple & dargah atop hill :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Ausa Fort

Historic post‑Bahamani fortress featuring moat and Pani Mahal, 20 km from city centre :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Ganj Golai

Circular marketplace built c.1917 around the Jagdamba temple, with 16 radiating streets—a commercial heart :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.

Culture & Economy

Latur is known for its vibrant celebration of festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali and Holi, and hosts cultural events like the Latur Utsav :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.

The local economy is driven by education, agriculture (sugarcane, soybean, grapes, mangoes) and industries. It's part of Maharashtra's 'Sugar Belt' and hosts MIDC zones, grape wine parks, and a proposed food park :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
